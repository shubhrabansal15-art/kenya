Carbon Efficiency
Carbon Efficiency Ratio
GDP per capita ÷ CO₂ per capita (USD / tonne) · selected year

CER measures economic value generated per tonne of per-capita emissions. Unlike aggregate efficiency, this is population-neutral — a fair basis for comparing economies of different sizes. Kenya's high CER relative to income peers confirms that its clean electricity mix is a structural advantage, not an artefact of low industrialisation alone.

Economic Efficiency
GDP generated per tonne CO₂ (USD/t) · selected year

Higher values mean more economic output per unit of carbon — a measure of green productivity. Economies with service-heavy or renewable-powered sectors tend to score higher. This metric complements CER by using total rather than per-capita emissions as the denominator.
